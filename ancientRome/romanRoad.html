The Roman Road System

By Amit Yadin and Emma Goldberg

Introduction

The Roman road system was one of the greatest engineering achievements of the ancient world. It connected cities, provinces, and military bases across the Roman Empire.

These roads helped Rome control its territory, grow economically, and spread its culture. Many Roman roads are still used today.

Why Roman Roads Were Important

  • Fast military movement
  • Improved communication
  • Boosted trade
  • Strengthened Roman control

Who Built the Roads?

Roman soldiers built most roads during times of peace. Engineers planned routes while soldiers and laborers completed construction.

How Roman Roads Were Built

  1. Land cleared and leveled
  2. Trench dug for stability
  3. Stone foundation laid
  4. Gravel added
  5. Paving stones placed

Military Importance

Roads allowed legions to march up to 30 miles per day and respond quickly to threats.

Trade & Economy

Merchants transported food, metals, pottery, and textiles across the empire.

Cultural Influence

Roman language, architecture, religion, and ideas spread through travel.

Legacy

Many modern highways still follow Roman routes today.
