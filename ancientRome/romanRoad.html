Arch of Titus

Historical Background

The Arch of Titus was built in Rome around 81 CE near the Roman Forum.

  • Ancient Rome
  • Built in 81 CE
  • Near Roman Forum
  • Triumphal arch

Reason for Construction

Built to celebrate Emperor Titus’s victory in Jerusalem in 70 CE.

  • Honors Titus
  • Victory in Jerusalem
  • End of revolt
  • Roman propaganda

Arch Builders

Commissioned by Emperor Domitian after Titus’s death.

  • Ordered by Domitian
  • Roman architects
  • Made of marble

Design of Arch

A single arch with Corinthian columns and detailed relief carvings.

  • Single arch
  • Corinthian columns
  • Relief sculptures
  • Symmetrical design

Relief on Arch

Shows Roman soldiers carrying treasures and Titus in a triumphal scene.
